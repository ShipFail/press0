Writing is hard. Publishing shouldn’t be harder.

Yesterday’s story: an author finishes a deeply personal biography in Markdown, publishes it as a blog post, falls in love with the words—and then spends **dozens of hours** fighting LaTeX, bleed, margins, and cover alignment just to get a single KDP-ready PDF.

The emotional arc is always the same: joy at finishing the writing, followed by frustration, delay, and pixel-pushing. Traditional tools assume you are a production engineer, a print technician, and a designer. Most authors just want their book to **look like a real book.**

Press0 exists to remove that gap. It is the invisible publishing studio that sits between “I’m done writing” and “My book is on a shelf.”

One flow. From draft to press.

Under the hood, Press0 coordinates layout, typography, illustration, and print QA so you never have to think about LaTeX, trims, or bleed again.

1

Drop in your words.

Markdown, DOCX, Notion export, blog archives—Press0 ingests your long-form writing and maps it into chapters, sections, and narrative beats.

2

Let the agents design.

AI layout agents choose typography, build chapter hero openers, generate illustration if needed, and set up geometry that matches real printers.

3

Download your master copy.

Press0 outputs a press-ready PDF. Margins, bleed, DPI, and fonts are validated. You can send it straight to KDP, Ingram, or your local press.

Powered by the Press0 Protocol.

Underneath the calm surface of the UI, Press0 runs a simple idea: every book can be described as a clear specification, then rendered perfectly.

The Press0 Protocol reads your manuscript, infers structure and style, encodes it as a machine-readable book spec, then drives layout, illustration, and print checks from that single source of truth.
